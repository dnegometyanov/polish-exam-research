## 1. Exam Overview

### What is it?
The **Egzamin certyfikatowy z jezyka polskiego jako obcego** (State Certificate Examination in Polish as a Foreign Language) is administered by the Polish State Commission for the Certification of Proficiency in Polish as a Foreign Language, under the authority of NAWA (Narodowa Agencja Wymiany Akademickiej). The certificate is legally required for obtaining Polish citizenship and EU long-term residence permits (minimum B1 level).

### Exam Structure
The exam is conducted over **two days** (typically Saturday + Sunday):

**Day 1 -- Written Part (190 minutes total):**

| Module | Duration | Points | Tasks |
|--------|----------|--------|-------|
| Listening (Rozumienie ze sluchu) | 30 min | 30 | 5 tasks |
| Reading (Rozumienie tekstow pisanych) | 40 min | 30 | 5 tasks |
| Grammar (Poprawnosc gramatyczna) | 45 min | 30 | 8 tasks |
| Writing (Pisanie) | 75 min | 30 | 2 tasks |

**Day 2 -- Oral Part (Mowienie):**

| Component | Duration | Points |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Speaking (3 tasks) | max 15 min | 40 |

15-20 minute breaks between written modules.

### Passing Requirements
- **Minimum 50% in EACH module** (you cannot compensate a weak section with a strong one)
- Results announced approximately 1.5-2 months after the exam
- Certificate issued about 1 month after results

### 2026 Exam Sessions
| Session | Dates |
|---------|-------|
| 1 | February 14-15 |
| 2 | April 25-26 |
| 3 | June 27-28 |
| 4 | October 17-18 |
| 5 | December 5-6 |

### Fees
- Adults (B1): **150 EUR**
- Youth 14-17 (B1): **90 EUR**
- Certificate issuance: **20 EUR**

### Registration
- Opens **2 months** before the exam date, open for 1 month
- **WARNING**: Spots fill within minutes of registration opening -- monitor test center websites closely
- Official registration info: https://certyfikatpolski.pl

### Permitted Materials
- Two non-erasable pens (black or blue)
- Water
- Identification document

## 5. Grammar Resources

### Essential B1 Grammar Topics

**Cases (Przypadki)** -- THE critical grammar area:
1. Mianownik (Nominative) -- subject
2. Dopelniacz (Genitive) -- possession, negation, quantities
3. Celownik (Dative) -- indirect object
4. Biernik (Accusative) -- direct object
5. Narzednik (Instrumental) -- "with," professions
6. Miejscownik (Locative) -- location, "about"
7. Wolacz (Vocative) -- addressing someone

All cases must be mastered for: nouns, adjectives, pronouns, and numerals (both singular and plural).

**Verb System:**
- Present tense conjugation (regular + irregular verbs)
- Past tense (including gendered endings: -li for masculine personal, -ly for non-masculine personal)
- Future tense (compound and simple)
- Conditional mood (tryb warunkowy)
- Imperative mood
- **Aspect** (aspekt) -- perfective vs imperfective distinction
  - Key prefixes: po-, na-, wy-, za-, prze-, etc.
- Reflexive verbs (myc sie, uczyc sie, etc.)

**Other Grammar Areas:**
- Comparative and superlative adjectives/adverbs
- Prepositions with correct case government
- Negation (nie, nikt, nic, nigdy, nigdzie)
- Relative clauses (ktory, ktora, ktore)
- Question formation (yes/no and information questions)
- Word order in complex sentences
- Conjunctions and subordinate clauses

### Most Difficult Grammar Area (per exam lecturers)
The most challenging task on the exam is choosing the correct verb tense AND writing the proper conjugated form. Even confident speakers struggle with this.

## 7. Writing Preparation

### Section Structure
- **Duration**: 75 minutes
- **Tasks**: 2 texts (one short form + one long form)
- **Selection**: Choose 1 of 3 provided sets; must complete BOTH tasks from chosen set
- **Points**: 30

### Short Form Texts (25-50 words)
Types that may appear:

| Type | Polish | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Greetings | Pozdrowienia | Postcard from vacation |
| Wishes | Zyczenia | Birthday, holidays, new job |
| Invitation | Zaproszenie | Party, exhibition, dinner |
| Announcement | Ogloszenie | Selling apartment, job posting |
| Notification | Zawiadomienie | School meeting, event notice |
| Congratulations | Gratulacje | Achievement, promotion |
| Thank you | Podziekowanie | For a gift, for help |
| Apology | Przeprosiny | For being late, for a mistake |

### Long Form Texts (150-175 words)
Types that may appear:

| Type | Polish | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Informal letter | List nieformalny | To a friend about vacation |
| Formal letter | List formalny | Complaint, request |
| Description of a place | Opis miejsca | Favorite vacation spot |
| Description of a person | Opis osoby | Best friend, teacher |
| Description of an object | Opis przedmiotu | Artwork, item |
| Character sketch | Charakterystyka | Of an actor or public figure |
| Narrative/Story | Opowiadanie | Fictional story |
| Report | Sprawozdanie | From a trip or event |
| Review | Recenzja | Of a book or film |
| Essay | Rozprawka | Opinion piece with arguments |

### Preparation Strategy
1. **Do NOT try to prepare all types** -- focus on the most common ones
2. **Always-recurring types** to master first:
   - Wishes (zyczenia) -- birthday, vacation greetings
   - Invitation (zaproszenie)
   - Review (recenzja) -- appears in both writing AND speaking
   - Informal letter
3. Learn the **required structure** for each text type (each has a specific form)
4. Memorize key phrases and connectors
5. Practice word count management (staying within limits)
6. Proofread carefully -- spelling and case endings matter

### Key Writing Resources
- **"Pisz po polsku!"** -- dedicated textbook for B1 writing preparation
  - Clear language, intuitive exercises
  - Templates for all text types
  - Available at: https://ksiegarnia.poltax.waw.pl/
- **"B1 - Pisze, mowie i zdaje!"** -- forms of written and oral expression for the certification exam

## 8. Speaking Preparation

### Section Structure
- **Duration**: max 15 minutes
- **Preparation time**: up to 5 minutes after receiving the task set
- **Selection**: Candidate randomly draws 1 of 3 exam sets
- **Points**: 40 (highest-weighted section!)
- **Format**: Face-to-face with examiners

### Three Speaking Tasks

**Task 1: Photo Description (Opis zdjecia)**
- Describe what you see in a photograph
- Who is in the photo, what are they doing, where are they
- Speculate about the context

**Task 2: Monologue on a Given Topic**
- Prepared speech on a specific topic
- Topics relate to everyday life themes (see vocabulary areas)
- May include: describing experiences, expressing opinions, talking about plans
- Could be a book/film review (overlaps with writing prep!)

**Task 3: Dialogue with the Examiner**
- Interactive conversation following task instructions
- Role-play scenarios (e.g., making arrangements, solving problems)
- Expressing and defending opinions

### Common Speaking Topics
- Daily life and routines
- Work and education
- Travel and holidays
- Food and cooking
- Health and fitness
- Technology and internet
- Culture and entertainment
- Family and relationships
- Environment and seasons
- City life vs. countryside

### Preparation Strategies
1. **Practice with another person** -- this is essential, not optional
2. Learn structured phrases for:
   - Describing photos: "Na zdjeciu widze...", "W tle jest...", "Osoba na zdjeciu wyglada na..."
   - Expressing opinions: "Moim zdaniem...", "Uwazam, ze...", "Zgadzam sie / Nie zgadzam sie"
   - Connecting ideas: "Po pierwsze..., po drugie...", "Z jednej strony..., z drugiej strony..."
   - Agreeing/disagreeing in dialogue
3. Prepare a "review template" (can use for both writing and speaking)
4. Practice under timed conditions (5 min prep + delivery)
5. Examiners are reportedly "nice" -- do not panic

### Speaking Resources
- Speaking practice with tutors (iTalki, Preply, course tutors)
- "B1 - Pisze, mowie i zdaje!" textbook
- Busuu app community for speaking feedback
- Talkpal AI for conversation practice

## 9. Preparation Timeline

### From Zero to B1
If starting from scratch: **approximately 350-500 hours** of study to reach B1 level (per CEFR estimates for Polish, a Category IV language).

### From A2 to B1
With existing A2 knowledge: **6-12 months** of regular study.

### Exam-Specific Preparation (Already at B1 level)
If you are already roughly at B1 level and need to prepare specifically for the exam format:

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **3-4 months before** | Confirm exam date, check registration schedule |
| **2-3 months before** | Register when registration opens (act fast -- spots fill in minutes!) |
| **2 months before** | Start structured preparation: daily/near-daily practice |
| **6-10 weeks before** | Begin working through sample exams systematically |
| **4-6 weeks before** | Join a preparation course if desired |
| **2-3 weeks before** | Intensify mock tests, especially listening and speaking |
| **1 week before** | Full mock exam under timed conditions; review weak areas |

### Formal Course Durations
- VARIA: 4 weeks (1x/week, 3 hours)
- Together School: 4-6 weeks (2-3x/week, 90 min)
- Vitas School: ~50 hours total
- "Zdaj sie na polski!" textbook: designed for minimum 120 hours of work
- Weekend intensive: ~1 month (8 meetings x 3 hours)

### Recommended Daily Study Approach
- 30-60 min daily minimum
- Alternate between skills: grammar drills, reading, listening, writing practice
- Weekly speaking practice session with a partner or tutor
- Complete 1 full practice exam per week in the final month
- Track common mistakes and review them

## 11. Adults vs Youth Exam

### Age Groups
- **Adults (Dorosi)**: 18+ years old on the exam day
- **Youth (Mlodzez)**: 14-17 years old (requires parental/guardian written consent)

### Available Levels
| Group | Available Levels |
|-------|-----------------|
| Adults | A2, B1, B2, C1, C2 |
| Youth (14-17) | A2, B1, B2 |

### Key Differences
- **Fee**: Adults 150 EUR vs Youth 90 EUR (at B1/B2)
- **Content**: Youth exam is "adapted to the needs of children and youth" (dostosowany do potrzeb dzieci i mlodziezy) -- topics and contexts are age-appropriate
- **Exam structure**: Same overall format (written + oral) but with different topics and texts suitable for younger candidates
- **Scheduling**: Not all exam dates offer both adult and youth versions -- check specific sessions
- **Certificate**: Both versions grant the same official state certificate

### Who Takes Which?
- If you are 18+ on exam day: adult version only
- If you are 14-17: youth version only (with parental consent)
- Under 14: not eligible
