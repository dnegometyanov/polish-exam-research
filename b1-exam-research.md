# Egzamin certyfikatowy z jezyka polskiego jako obcego -- poziom B1

## Comprehensive Research Report

**Date compiled:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1. Who Administers the Exam

### Official Body
The exam is administered under the authority of the **Panstwowa Komisja ds. Poswiadczania Znajomosci Jezyka Polskiego jako Obcego** (State Commission for the Certification of Proficiency in Polish as a Foreign Language).

The certification system is overseen by **NAWA -- Narodowa Agencja Wymiany Akademickiej** (Polish National Agency for Academic Exchange), based at Polna 40, 00-635 Warszawa.
- Email: certyfikacja@nawa.gov.pl
- Official website: https://certyfikatpolski.pl

### Examination Centers
The exam is conducted at authorized examination centers (centra egzaminacyjne) throughout Poland and abroad. A current list is available on certyfikatpolski.pl. Notable centers include universities such as UMCS Lublin, UG Gdansk, and private language schools accredited for this purpose.

### Who Can Take the Exam
- Foreign nationals (cudzoziemcy)
- Polish citizens permanently residing abroad
- Candidates must be at least **18 years old** on the day of the exam
- Valid passport required as identification

### Registration Process
1. Complete a preliminary registration form on the chosen examination center's website
2. Wait for confirmation of available spots
3. Receive payment details with a deadline
4. Pay the fee on time (reservation forfeited if missed)
5. Registration confirmed only after payment is received
- **Registration closes one month before the scheduled exam date**

### Fees (B1 level, adults)
- **Exam fee:** maximum **150 EUR** (each center sets its own fee within this cap)
- **Certificate issuance fee:** **20 EUR** (charged only if you pass)
- Some centers quote prices in PLN (approx. 700 PLN + 100 PLN for certificate)

### Exam Dates in 2026
| Date | Levels offered |
|------|---------------|
| 14-15 February 2026 | B1, B2 (adults and children/youth) |
| 25-26 April 2026 | B1, B2, C1 (adults) |
| 27-28 June 2026 | B1, B2 (adults) |
| 17-18 October 2026 | B1, C1 (adults) |
| 5-6 December 2026 | B1 (adults) |

### Results and Certificate Timeline
- Results arrive via email approximately **2-3 months** after the exam
- Certificate delivery takes approximately **16-20 weeks** (about 6 months total)
- The examination center reviews papers in approx. 1 month, then NAWA audits

## 2. Official Exam Structure -- Overview

The B1 exam consists of **two main parts** divided into **5 modules** total:

| Part | Modules | Total Duration |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **Written (czesc pisemna)** | 4 modules | up to **190 minutes** |
| **Oral (czesc ustna)** | 1 module | up to **15 minutes** (+5 min prep) |

The written part is typically held on **Saturday** and the oral part on **Sunday**, though some centers offer both on the same day.

There are approximately 15-20 minute breaks between written modules.

### Permitted Materials
- Two non-erasable pens (black or blue)
- Water
- Identification document (passport)

## 3. Detailed Breakdown of Each Section

---

### 3.1 Rozumienie ze sluchu (Listening Comprehension)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Duration** | approx. 30 minutes |
| **Number of tasks** | 5 |
| **Maximum points** | 30 |
| **Passing threshold** | 15 points (50%) |

#### Task descriptions:

**Zadanie 1 -- Short utterances (multiple choice a/b/c)**
- Listen to very short spoken utterances
- Choose the correct answer (a, b, or c)
- Determine: who is the speaker, what is the meaning, what type of utterance, or where it might be heard
- **Played ONCE only** (this is the hardest task)

**Zadanie 2 -- Short dialogues (multiple choice a/b/c)**
- Listen to very short dialogues
- Choose the correct answer from three options
- **Played TWICE**

**Zadanie 3 -- Longer text (true/false: TAK/NIE)**
- Listen to a longer monologue or dialogue (e.g., an interview)
- Decide whether given statements are consistent with the text (TAK) or not (NIE)
- Mark answers in a table
- **Played TWICE**

**Zadanie 4 -- Four speakers (matching)**
- Listen to utterances from four different people sharing opinions/information
- Match speakers (1-4) to specific statements
- One speaker may match multiple items; column matching format
- **Played TWICE**

**Zadanie 5 -- Matching to photographs**
- Match spoken utterances to photographs
- Considered the easiest task by many test-takers
- **Played TWICE**

**Notes:** Each task includes a worked example. No points for unanswered items.

---

### 3.2 Rozumienie tekstow pisanych (Reading Comprehension)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Duration** | 40-45 minutes |
| **Number of tasks** | 4-5 |
| **Maximum points** | 30 |
| **Passing threshold** | 15 points (50%) |

#### What is tested:
- Understanding of individual signs, single-paragraph text fragments, and complete short texts
- Pragmatic intentions of the text and its main idea (global comprehension)
- Extracting specific information from clearly structured texts (selective and detailed comprehension)
- Both printed and handwritten (neat, legible) texts

#### Task types encountered:

**Multiple choice (wybor wielokrotny)**
- Read text fragments and select the correct answer from options (a/b/c)

**True/False (TAK/NIE)**
- Read a text, then mark statements as YES (consistent with text) or NO (inconsistent)

**Matching (laczenie/dopasowywanie)**
- Connect titles from one column with text fragments from another column
- Write answers in a provided table

**Text completion (uzupelnianie tekstu)**
- Fill in missing words or choose correct forms to complete a text

**Text reconstruction (rekonstrukcja tekstu)**
- Arrange provided text fragments into a logical whole

#### Text sources:
Texts may come from newspapers (e.g., Gazeta Wyborcza, Dziennik Polski), fragments of literature, or popular science publications. All texts are adapted to B1 level vocabulary and structure and are culturally neutral.

### 3.3 Poprawnosc gramatyczna (Grammar)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Duration** | 45 minutes |
| **Number of tasks** | 8 |
| **Maximum points** | 30 |
| **Passing threshold** | 15 points (50%) |

#### The 8 tasks in detail (based on official sample tests and HelloPolish analysis):

**Zadanie 1 -- Noun, adjective & pronoun declension (odmiana rzeczownikow, przymiotnikow i zaimkow)**
- Instruction: "Prosze zaznaczyc poprawne formy wyrazow podanych w nawiasach"
- Mark the correct forms of words given in parentheses
- Tests complex declension: przyjaciel/przyjaciele, czlowiek/ludzie, tydzien/tygodnie
- Format: select from options provided in context

**Zadanie 2 -- Conjunctions (spojniki)**
- Instruction: "Prosze uzupelnic zdania wyrazami z ramki"
- Complete sentences with conjunctions from a provided box
- Word box example: ze | oraz | ale | chociaz | czyli | ani | wiec
- One word in the box is unnecessary (distractor)

**Zadanie 3 -- Comparison of adjectives & adverbs (stopniowanie przymiotnikow i przyslowkow)**
- Instruction: "Prosze zaznaczyc poprawne formy wyrazow podanych w nawiasach"
- Select appropriate comparative/superlative forms
- E.g., duzy -- wiekszy -- najwiekszy; duzo -- wiecej -- najwiecej

**Zadanie 4 -- Verb tenses: present & future (czas terazniejszy i przyszly)**
- Instruction: "Prosze uzupelnic tekst poprawnymi formami wyrazow podanych w nawiasach"
- Create correct present or future tense forms from given infinitives
- Tests both simple future (pojde, zjemy) and compound future (bede studiowala)

**Zadanie 5 -- Question formation (tworzenie pytan)**
- Given a sentence with an underlined element, form a question about that element
- Example: "Marek zrobi zakupy jutro" (jutro underlined) -> "Kiedy Marek zrobi zakupy?"

**Zadanie 6 -- Sentence transformation (przeksztalcanie zdan)**
- Rewrite a sentence using a provided word while maintaining the original meaning
- Example: "Bylismy w teatrze" + word "do" -> "Poszlismy do teatru"

**Zadanie 7 -- Verb aspect & mood (aspekt i tryby czasownika)**
- Select correct verb forms in context
- Tests imperative mood (Zrob to!, Chodzmy!) and conditional mood (Gdybym byl zdrowy, wyszedlbym na dwor)
- Also tests perfective vs. imperfective aspect choices

**Zadanie 8 -- Prepositions (przyimki)**
- Instruction: "Prosze uzupelnic zdania wyrazami z ramki"
- Insert prepositions from a provided list into text
- Preposition examples: do, dla, za, po, w, na
- One preposition in the box is unnecessary (distractor)

#### Comprehensive grammar topics tested:

**Nouns (Rzeczowniki):**
- Declension in singular and plural for all genders
- Feminine nouns ending in: -a, -i, consonants (mama, pani, milosc, noc, rzecz)
- Masculine nouns: consonant-ending (rower, samochod) and vowel-ending (kolega, artysta)
- Neuter nouns (miasto, serce, muzeum)
- Irregular declension (dzien, tydzien, rok, przyjaciel, czlowiek)
- Consonant alternations during declension (duze miasto vs. duzym miescie)
- Proper nouns: city names, surnames ending in -ski/-ska/-cki/-cka

**Adjectives (Przymiotniki):**
- Full declension in singular and plural
- Comparative forms (drogi-drozszy-najdrozszy; maly-mniejszy-najmniejszy)
- Agreement with noun gender and case

**Pronouns (Zaimki):**
- Personal: ja, ty, on, ona, ono, my, wy, oni, one
- Demonstrative: ten, ta, to
- Possessive: moj/moja/moje, twoj/twoja/twoje, nasz/nasza/nasze
- Indefinite: ktos, cos
- Negative: nikt, nic
- Interrogative: kto?, co?, jaki?, ktory?, czyj?

**Numerals (Liczebniki):**
- Cardinal numerals in nominative (jeden/jedna/jedno, dwa/dwie, piec, siedem, osiem)
- Ordinal numerals with gender agreement (pierwszy/pierwsza/pierwsze)
- Note: cardinal numerals are B1-level but currently lack dedicated exam tasks

**Verbs (Czasowniki):**
- Present tense: all conjugation patterns (mieszkam, umiem, lubie, tancze, biore)
- Past tense: perfective and imperfective, with gendered plural endings (-li vs. -ly)
- Simple future (pojde, zjemy, wrocicie)
- Compound future (bede studiowala, bedziemy pracowac)
- Imperative mood (Zrob to!, Chodzmy!)
- Conditional mood (Gdybym byl bogaty, kupilbym nowe mieszkanie)
- Impersonal forms: mozna, warto, trzeba, nalezy
- Motion verbs (isc/chodzic, jechac/jezdzic)
- Verb government/rekcja (potrzebowac + genitive; myslec o + locative; wybierac + accusative)

**Adverbs (Przyslowki):**
- Formation from adjectives (wspanialy > wspaniale, dobry > dobrze)
- Comparative forms (dobrze-lepiej-najlepiej, zle-gorzej-najgorzej)

**Deverbal nouns (Rzeczowniki odczasownikowe):**
- gotowanie, sprzatanie, pisanie (neuter gender)

**Syntax (Skladnia):**
- Complex sentences with conjunctions: a, i, zeby, albo, lecz, ale, poniewaz, dlatego, choc, chociaz, jednak, wiec, ze, aby
- Prepositions with correct case governance

**Conjunctions tested:** ze, zeby, bo, wiec, ale, ani, oraz, chociaz, czyli, poniewaz, dlatego

### 3.4 Pisanie (Writing)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Duration** | 75 minutes |
| **Number of tasks** | 2 texts (from 1 chosen set) |
| **Maximum points** | 30 |
| **Passing threshold** | 15 points (50%) |

#### Format:
- Candidates choose **1 set out of 3 available sets**
- Each set contains **2 writing tasks** (Text A and Text B)
- You must complete **both texts** from your chosen set
- Topics are formulated so they do NOT require specialist knowledge

#### Text A -- Short form (krotka forma)
- **Length:** 25-50 words
- **Possible formats:**
  - Ogloszenie (announcement/classified ad) -- e.g., selling a car, renting an apartment, lost & found
  - Zaproszenie (invitation)
  - Pozdrowienia z wakacji (holiday greetings / postcard)
  - Zyczenia (wishes -- birthday, holiday, etc.)

#### Text B -- Long form (dluzsza forma)
- **Length:** 150-175 words
- **Possible formats:**
  - List (letter) -- formal or informal (e.g., to a friend)
  - E-mail
  - Recenzja (review -- book, film, restaurant)
  - Opis (description -- a person, a dream apartment, a place)
  - Relacja (account -- vacation, event)
  - Charakterystyka (character description of a close person)
  - Rozprawka (essay -- expressing opinions with arguments)
  - Opowiadanie (short narrative/fictional story)

**Note on word limits:** A 10% margin of error applies -- texts slightly exceeding or falling short of the target won't lose points for length alone.

#### Specific example writing prompts (from official sample tests):

**Short text (Text A) examples:**
- Write a postcard with holiday greetings from your vacation
- Write birthday wishes to a Polish friend
- Write an invitation to a barbecue for new work colleagues
- Write an announcement about selling your apartment
- Write a notice about a school meeting

**Long text (Text B) examples:**
- Write a letter to a Polish friend about your vacation in your home country
- Write an email informing a friend who is getting married that you cannot attend the celebration
- Write a review of a book or film you recently read/saw
- Write an essay: "Internet jest niezbedny w dzisiejszym zyciu" (Internet is essential in today's life) -- present arguments for and against
- Write an essay about the influence of television on children
- Write a description of a person particularly close to you
- Write an account of a difficult personal situation and how you handled it
- Write about your experience of life in Poland

**Essay structure requirements:** Introduction (wstep) + development (rozwiazanie) + conclusion (zakonczenie). Must contain: author's opinions, commentary, arguments supporting those views.

**Letter structure requirements:** Proper greeting/address to recipient (Drogi/Droga... or Szanowny Panie...), body, closing (Z pozdrowionami / Z powazaniem), signature.

#### Key evaluation criteria:
- Writing on the assigned topic (nie odbiegac od tematu)
- Staying within word limits
- Maintaining proper form and structure (introduction, body, conclusion)
- Appropriate register (formal vs. informal)
- Grammatical accuracy and vocabulary range
- Each text type has determined forms and specific structures that must appear

### 3.5 Mowienie (Speaking)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Duration** | up to 15 minutes |
| **Preparation time** | approx. 5 minutes |
| **Number of tasks** | 3 |
| **Maximum points** | 40 |
| **Passing threshold** | 20 points (50%) |

#### Format:
- Candidate draws **1 set out of 3 available sets**
- Each set contains **3 tasks** based on diverse materials (texts and illustrations)
- Exam is conducted before an examination panel (komisja egzaminacyjna)
- Notes may be taken during preparation time

#### Task descriptions:

**Zadanie 1 -- Photo description (opis zdjecia) -- Monologue**
- Describe a photograph as accurately and in as much detail as possible
- Include: people (appearance, clothing, actions, emotions), setting (interior/exterior, weather, objects)
- Use spatial language: "Na pierwszym planie..." (In the foreground), "Na drugim planie..." (In the background), "Z lewej/prawej strony..." (On the left/right side)

**Zadanie 2 -- Monologue on a given topic (wypowiedz na zadany temat)**
- Express personal opinions or describe something significant
- Example topics from official sample tests:
  - "Jakie sa skuteczne metody uczenia sie jezykow obcych? Prosze przedstawic swoja opinie na ten temat." (What are effective methods for learning foreign languages?)
  - "Prosze opisac osobe, ktora jest Pani/Panu szczegolnie bliska. Prosze uzasadnic swoj wybor." (Describe a person particularly close to you. Justify your choice.)
  - Advantages of living in a city vs. countryside
  - Objects you could not live without
  - Your dream vacation

**Zadanie 3 -- Dialogue with the examiner (dialog z egzaminatorem)**
- Role-play a conversation based on a given scenario
- Examiner takes on a designated role
- Example scenarios from official sample tests:
  - Calling a language school to inquire about courses, timing, pricing, availability
  - Calling a sick friend to check on them and offer to go shopping for them
  - Ordering items at a bakery
  - Making a complaint at a hotel/restaurant
  - Arranging a meeting with a colleague
- **Important:** Determine whether the situation calls for formal (Pan/Pani) or informal (ty) register

#### Detailed assessment criteria (40 points total):

**Task completion (Wykonanie zadan) -- 20 points:**
- Zadanie 1 (opis fotografii / photo description): 0-7 points
- Zadanie 2 (monolog): 0-7 points
- Zadanie 3 (sytuacja komunikacyjna / dialogue): 0-6 points

**Language accuracy (Poprawnosc jezykowa) -- 20 points:**
- Gramatyka (grammar): 0-8 points
- Slownictwo i styl (vocabulary and style): 0-8 points
- Poprawnosc fonetyczna i plynnosc wypowiedzi (phonetic accuracy and fluency): 0-4 points

**Note:** Examiners are generally described as friendly and supportive (nastawieni przyjazne).

## 4. Scoring System -- Summary

### Points per section (adults)

| Module | Max Points |
|--------|-----------|
| Rozumienie ze sluchu (Listening) | 30 |
| Rozumienie tekstow pisanych (Reading) | 30 |
| Poprawnosc gramatyczna (Grammar) | 30 |
| Pisanie (Writing) | 30 |
| Mowienie (Speaking) | 40 |
| **TOTAL** | **160** |

Written part total: 120 points
Oral part total: 40 points

### Passing Criteria
- **Minimum 50% in EACH individual module**
- This means you need at least:
  - 15/30 in Listening
  - 15/30 in Reading
  - 15/30 in Grammar
  - 15/30 in Writing
  - 20/40 in Speaking
- **You cannot compensate a failed module with a strong result in another module**
- If you fail even one module, you fail the entire exam

### Minimum total to pass: 80/160 (if exactly 50% in every module)

## 5. Scoring for Children and Youth Version (ages 14-17)

For reference, the adapted version for younger candidates has a different structure:

| Module | Duration | Tasks | Points |
|--------|----------|-------|--------|
| Listening | 30 min | 5 | 30 |
| Reading with Grammar Recognition | 60 min | 7 | 40 |
| Writing | 60 min | 2 | 30 |
| **Written total** | **150 min** | **14** | **100** |
| Speaking | 15 min | 2 | 40 |
| **GRAND TOTAL** | | | **140** |

Passing: minimum 50% on written section AND 50% on oral section.

**Note:** As of 2025, some centers no longer offer the children/youth format.

## 7. Common Difficulties Reported by Test-Takers

### 7.1 Hardest Sections (Consensus)

Based on all collected accounts, the sections ranked by difficulty:

1. **Grammar -- Universally considered the hardest.** The Chido-Fajny blogger rated it 10/10 difficulty and barely passed (50%). The Polonization blogger described it as "just fucking tough." Even language professionals and examiners acknowledge its difficulty. One professional admitted uncertainty about whether they would pass it themselves.

2. **Writing -- Second hardest.** Examiners confirm that "the most difficult part for candidates is grammar and writing." The Prolog Publishing blog notes that many candidates struggle not with *how* to write (vocabulary/grammar) but *what* to write (content/ideas). Additional challenges include:
   - Handwriting unfamiliarity -- many candidates rarely write by hand, even in their native language
   - Polish punctuation rules are almost never taught in A1-B1 textbooks
   - Only two dedicated resources exist for teaching Polish orthography to foreigners
   - The longer text (170 words) causes particular stress

3. **Listening (Task 1 specifically) -- A notorious trap.** The very first task of the entire exam is played only once and requires identifying where short utterances would be heard or what type of message they are. Multiple sources describe this as "HARD."

4. **Speaking -- Moderately difficult but manageable.** Examiners are consistently described as friendly. The stress comes from nervousness rather than task difficulty. Key advice: never stop talking, even if making errors.

5. **Reading -- Generally considered the easiest.** Both bloggers who shared scores achieved 90-100%.

### 7.2 Specific Grammar Traps

- Conjugating irregular verbs incorrectly
- Declining adjectives incorrectly
- Misusing verb aspects (dokonany vs. niedokonany)
- Wrong case after prepositions
- Gender-specific verb endings: "-li" (masculine personal, group with at least one man) vs. "-ly" (non-masculine personal, all women/children/objects/animals)
- Choosing the correct tense and writing the proper verb form -- described as "the most challenging task" by lecturers

### 7.3 Writing-Specific Challenges

- Word count discipline: a 10% margin applies, but exceeding or falling short may cost points or lead to disqualification
- Not understanding the required text format (e.g., knowing what a "recenzja" vs. a "relacja" looks like)
- Polish punctuation rules not covered in standard textbooks
- Physical unfamiliarity with handwriting (candidates accustomed only to typing)

## 8. Tips from Successful Candidates

### 8.1 Preparation Strategy

1. **Take a diagnostic test first.** Download free practice tests from certyfikatpolski.pl, simulate exam conditions with a timer, and identify your weak areas. Aim for 70%+ on self-administered tests before investing in courses. (Source: Pozdrowienia z Polski instructor)

2. **Invest in exam-specific preparation, not just general Polish classes.** Multiple successful candidates stress that general language lessons are insufficient. Exam preparation courses teach the specific formats, time constraints, and strategies needed. (Source: Chido-Fajny, Polonization blogs)

3. **Use free official resources first.** The certyfikatpolski.pl website has free practice exams and task collections. These are considered better than most paid books. (Source: Polonization blog)

4. **Study 50 hours minimum for exam-specific preparation** on top of general language knowledge. (Source: Vitas School lecturer)

5. **Practice writing by hand** -- this is a skill many modern learners have lost even in their native language. (Source: Prolog Publishing)

6. **The 90/10 rule:** "My help is 10%, your independent work is 90%." Self-study is the main driver of success. (Source: Pozdrowienia z Polski instructor)

### 8.2 Section-Specific Strategies

**Listening:**
- Practice Task 1 (played once) extensively -- it is the first thing on the entire exam and sets the tone
- The remaining tasks are played twice and are "fairly simple"
- Get comfortable with natural-speed spoken Polish through podcasts and radio

**Reading:**
- Practice even if it feels like your strongest area
- Approach texts like detective work -- actively search for key information
- Use newspaper articles from Gazeta Wyborcza for practice

**Grammar:**
- Focus on: all 7 cases, verb aspects, comparatives/superlatives, conditionals, prepositions + correct case
- "There's no getting around it" -- drill repeatedly
- Grammar exercises can challenge even confident speakers

**Writing:**
- Focus strategically on recurring formats: wishes (zyczenia), greetings from vacation (pozdrowienia z wakacji), reviews (recenzje), and informal letters
- Learn the structure of each text type rather than memorizing content
- Count words in practice (or memorize approximately what 50 / 175 words looks like on paper)
- Points are awarded for correct text length

**Speaking:**
- Practice with another person -- never go to the oral exam unprepared
- During photo description: spend about 1 minute describing the image, then spend the remaining time discussing the topic/giving your opinion
- Use spatial vocabulary: "Na pierwszym planie...", "Na drugim planie...", "Z lewej/prawej strony..."
- Determine whether each scenario requires formal (Pan/Pani) or informal (ty) register
- Speaking slower when nervous helps avoid mistakes
- "Try not to get nervous, because then you more easily make mistakes. Examiners are friendly-oriented, so don't stress!"
- The introductory "small talk" is not graded, but "the first impression is very important" (Source: Polonijne.pl)

### 8.3 Exam Day Tips

1. Stop studying the day before -- avoid mental overload (Source: Chido-Fajny blog)
2. Arrive early for registration
3. Bring: passport, 2+ pens (non-erasable, black or blue), water, snacks
4. All instructions are in Polish -- there is no English on the exam
5. Phones are collected at the door
6. Expect 130+ candidates at popular centers (mostly from Ukraine and Belarus)
7. Protect sleep and stay hydrated -- "test anxiety is normal"
8. Reset mentally between sections

## 9. Time Management Issues

### 9.1 Section Time Allocations

| Section | Time | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Listening | ~30 min | Strictly controlled by audio playback; no time management possible |
| Reading | 40-45 min | Generally adequate if well-prepared |
| Grammar | 45 min | 8 tasks in 45 minutes can feel tight |
| Writing | 75 min | Two texts: must budget time between short (25-50 words) and long (150-175 words) |
| Speaking | 15 min | Strictly timed by examiners; ~5 min prep beforehand |

### 9.2 Time Pressure Points

- **Listening Task 1** -- No time to recover if you miss something; it is played once
- **Writing** -- The 75 minutes must cover both texts plus proofreading. The longer text (150-175 words) by hand takes significant time, especially for those not accustomed to handwriting
- **Grammar** -- 8 tasks in 45 minutes means approximately 5.5 minutes per task; some tasks (e.g., sentence transformation) require more thinking time

### 9.3 Advice on Time Management

- Practice with sample tests under strict timed conditions
- Learn to recognize approximately how many handwritten words fit on a page (to avoid counting during the writing section)
- Do not spend too long on any single grammar task -- move on and return if time permits
- 15-20 minute breaks between written modules provide some recovery time

## 10. Registration Difficulties

A recurring theme across all sources is the extreme difficulty of securing an exam spot:

- Spots sell out within **minutes** of registration opening. The Polonization blogger reported all spots gone by 13:34 -- "that's how fast it is."
- Some centers report spots gone in under **4 minutes**
- Registration opens approximately 2 months before the exam and closes 1 month before (or when full)
- Demand far outstrips supply, particularly at B1 level (driven by citizenship/residency applications)
- Some language schools now bundle exam registration with preparation courses, offering a "guaranteed spot" as a selling point
- If you fail, you must wait until the next available session (held 4-5 times per year), which may also be full
- At some centers, "it's not possible to register for the certification exam alone because the preliminary registration list is full" -- you must enroll in their prep course to get a spot

## 11. Alternative Exams

### TELC Polish B1-B2

As of 2023, the TELC exam is an officially recognized alternative to the state exam for EU long-term residence applications. Key differences:

| Feature | State Exam | TELC |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Frequency | 4-5 times/year | Flexible, set by schools independently |
| Registration | Extremely competitive | More available |
| Grammar section | 8 tasks, 45 min (standalone) | Only 2 grammar tasks |
| Writing | Multiple format types, handwritten | Semi-formal email only |
| Results timeline | 3-4 months | 4-5 weeks |
| Level | Single level (B1) | Dual level (B1-B2 in one sitting) |
| Focus | Strong grammar/writing emphasis | Communicative situations, speaking-focused |
| Recognized for | Citizenship + EU long-term residence | EU long-term residence (not citizenship) |

The TELC exam is described as "more friendly to candidates" due to the reduced grammar focus and communicative orientation. However, it is **not** accepted for Polish citizenship applications -- only the state exam (certyfikat polski) satisfies the citizenship language requirement.

**Source:** [Easy Polish Poznan -- How to Get a B1/B2 Certificate Quicker](https://easypolish.com.pl/en/blog-en/67-jak-szybciej-zdobyc-certyfikat-b1-z-jezyka-polskiego)

### ECL Exam

The ECL exam tests general Polish language skills for everyday and professional life. It focuses on practical communication and notably does **not** include a separate grammar section. Available at eclexam.eu.

## 12. 3 Myths About the B1 Exam (from Easy Polish Poznan)

**Source:** [3 Myths About the B1 Exam](https://easypolish.com.pl/en/blog-en/40-3-myths-about-the-b1-exam)

**Myth 1: "You can prepare for the exam in 3 months."**
Truth: Three months works only if you are already near B1 level. If you still struggle with grammar, have limited vocabulary, or weak writing skills, you need significantly more time.

**Myth 2: "I speak and understand Polish, so I'm ready!"**
Truth: Speaking and listening are only 2 of 5 exam sections. Grammar and writing consistently challenge candidates the most. Confusing cases or verb endings indicates you need more preparation.

**Myth 3: "The B1 exam is very difficult, I will never pass it!"**
Truth: While challenging, the exam is passable with patience. The recommended timeline is approximately 2 years from beginner level. One student succeeded in 9 months, but most people require longer due to work and family obligations.

## 13. Key Facts and Figures

- **Certificate validity:** The certificate has **no expiry date**
- **Exam cost:** EUR 150 (B1 adults) + EUR 20 for certificate
- **Pass rate:** Not officially published, but anecdotally grammar and writing are the most common failure points
- **Typical preparation time:** ~2 years from beginner; 6-12 months from A2; one student reportedly succeeded in 9 months from beginner
- **Results wait:** 2-4 months (varies by center and session); some report up to 6-7 months
- **Certificate delivery:** Approximately 6 months total from exam date
- **Exam requirement since:** February 2018 for permanent residence / citizenship applications
- **Only state-issued certificates count for citizenship:** "Any certificate issued by a private language school is completely worthless" for immigration purposes. TELC is accepted for EU long-term residence but not citizenship.
- **B1 level definition:** "B1 confirms functional independence, not native-like precision" -- examiners expect errors if communication is successful
- **Minimum age:** 18 for adult exam; 14 for youth version
