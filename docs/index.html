1. Exam Overview

What Is This Exam?

The State Certificate Examination in Polish as a Foreign Language is administered by the Państwowa Komisja ds. Poświadczania Znajomości Języka Polskiego jako Obcego, overseen by NAWA (Polish National Agency for Academic Exchange). The B1 certificate is legally required for:

  • Polish citizenship applications
  • EU long-term residence permits

Official website: certyfikatpolski.pl

Structure at a Glance

The exam spans two days (typically Saturday written + Sunday oral):

Day Module Duration Points Tasks
Day 1 Listening (Rozumienie ze słuchu) 30 min 30 5
Reading (Rozumienie tekstów pisanych) 40-45 min 30 4-5
Grammar (Poprawność gramatyczna) 45 min 30 8
Writing (Pisanie) 75 min 30 2
Day 2 Speaking (Mówienie) 15 min (+5 min prep) 40 3
TOTAL ~210 min 160

15-20 minute breaks between Day 1 modules.

Key Facts

  • Passing threshold: 50% in each module independently (no cross-compensation)
  • Certificate validity: Permanent (no expiry)
  • Cost: up to 150 EUR (exam) + 20 EUR (certificate issuance)
  • Results: 2-4 months after the exam
  • Everything is in Polish — no English instructions on the exam

2. Section-by-Section Breakdown with Examples

2.1 Listening (Rozumienie ze słuchu)

Parameter Value
Duration ~30 minutes
Tasks 5
Points 30
Pass minimum 15 points (50%)

Task Types

Task Format Played Difficulty
1 Short utterances → multiple choice (a/b/c): identify speaker, meaning, or context ONCE Hard
2 Short dialogues → multiple choice (a/b/c) Twice Medium
3 Longer monologue/interview → true/false (TAK/NIE) Twice Medium
4 Four speakers’ opinions → matching speakers to statements Twice Medium
5 Match utterances to photographs Twice Easy

Example (Task 1)

You hear a short announcement. You must choose where you might hear it: - a) w sklepie (in a shop) - b) na dworcu (at a station) - c) w szpitalu (in a hospital)

Warning: Task 1 is the very first thing on the entire exam, played only once, and is considered a notorious trap by test-takers. Practice it extensively.

2.2 Reading (Rozumienie tekstów pisanych)

Parameter Value
Duration 40-45 minutes
Tasks 4-5
Points 30
Pass minimum 15 points (50%)

Task Types

  • Multiple choice (a/b/c) — read text, select correct answer
  • True/False (TAK/NIE) — evaluate statements against the text
  • Matching — connect titles/headings with text fragments
  • Text completion — fill in missing words/phrases
  • Text reconstruction — arrange fragments into logical order

What Texts Look Like

Sources include adapted newspaper articles (e.g., Gazeta Wyborcza), popular science texts, and practical documents (announcements, ads, signs). All are culturally neutral and adapted to B1 vocabulary.

Candidate insight: Reading is consistently rated the easiest section. Both bloggers who shared scores achieved 90-100%. But don’t skip practice — “the actual test may be harder than expected.”

2.3 Grammar (Poprawność gramatyczna)

Parameter Value
Duration 45 minutes
Tasks 8
Points 30
Pass minimum 15 points (50%)

The 8 Tasks in Detail

Task What It Tests Format
1 Noun, adjective & pronoun declension Select correct forms from options
2 Conjunctions (spójniki) Fill in from a word box (one distractor)
3 Comparative/superlative adjectives & adverbs Select correct forms
4 Verb tenses: present & future Write correct forms from given infinitives
5 Question formation Form questions about underlined sentence elements
6 Sentence transformation Rewrite sentence using a provided word
7 Verb aspect & mood (imperative, conditional) Select correct verb forms
8 Prepositions (przyimki) Fill in from a word box (one distractor)

Examples

Task 5 — Question Formation: > Sentence: “Marek zrobi zakupy jutro.” (jutro underlined) > Answer: “Kiedy Marek zrobi zakupy?”

Task 6 — Sentence Transformation: > Original: “Byliśmy w teatrze.” + word: do > Answer: “Poszliśmy do teatru.”

Task 2 — Conjunctions (word box): > Box: że | oraz | ale | chociaż | czyli | ani | więc > “Lubię kawę, ___ nie piję jej wieczorem.” → ale

Essential Grammar Topics at B1

Category What You Must Know
Cases All 7 cases for nouns, adjectives, pronouns, numerals (singular + plural)
Verbs Present, past, future (simple + compound), conditional, imperative
Aspect Perfective vs. imperfective distinction (dokonany vs. niedokonany)
Motion verbs iść/chodzić, jechać/jeździć
Comparison Adjective and adverb comparative/superlative forms (including irregular: duży→większy, dobry→lepszy)
Prepositions Correct case government (do + Gen, na + Acc/Loc, w + Loc, z + Gen/Instr)
Pronouns Personal, possessive, demonstrative, interrogative, indefinite, negative
Syntax Complex sentences with: że, żeby, bo, więc, ale, ani, chociaż, ponieważ, dlatego
Impersonal można, warto, trzeba, należy
Verb government potrzebować + Gen; myśleć o + Loc; wybierać + Acc

Universal consensus: Grammar is the hardest section. One candidate rated it 10/10 difficulty, barely passing at 50%. Even language professionals admit uncertainty about passing it.

2.4 Writing (Pisanie)

Parameter Value
Duration 75 minutes
Tasks 2 texts (from 1 chosen set of 3)
Points 30
Pass minimum 15 points (50%)

Format

  • Choose 1 set out of 3 available
  • Each set has Text A (short) + Text B (long) — you must write both
  • Everything is handwritten

Text A — Short Form (25-50 words)

Type Polish Example Prompt
Holiday greetings Pozdrowienia Write a postcard from your vacation in Italy
Wishes Życzenia Write birthday wishes to your Polish friend Ania
Invitation Zaproszenie Invite colleagues to a barbecue at your house
Announcement Ogłoszenie Write an ad for selling your apartment
Congratulations Gratulacje Congratulate a friend on their wedding

Text B — Long Form (150-175 words)

Type Polish Example Prompt
Informal letter List nieformalny Write to a friend about your vacation in your home country
Formal letter List formalny Write a complaint about hotel service
Review Recenzja Write a review of a book/film you recently read/saw
Essay Rozprawka “Internet jest niezbędny w dzisiejszym życiu” — present arguments for and against
Description Opis Describe a person particularly close to you
Account/Report Sprawozdanie Describe a difficult situation and how you handled it

Structure Requirements

Essay: Introduction (wstęp) → Development with arguments (rozwinięcie) → Conclusion (zakończenie)

Letter: Greeting (Drogi/Droga… or Szanowny Panie…) → Body → Closing (Z pozdrowieniami / Z poważaniem) → Signature

10% word margin applies — slightly over/under won’t lose points, but significant deviation will.

2.5 Speaking (Mówienie)

Parameter Value
Duration max 15 minutes
Preparation ~5 minutes
Tasks 3
Points 40 (highest-weighted section!)
Pass minimum 20 points (50%)

The 3 Tasks

Task 1 — Photo Description (0-7 points) Describe a photograph in detail: people, actions, setting, emotions.

Useful phrases: - “Na zdjęciu widzę…” (In the photo I see…) - “Na pierwszym planie…” (In the foreground…) - “Na drugim planie…” / “W tle…” (In the background…) - “Z lewej/prawej strony…” (On the left/right side…) - “Osoba na zdjęciu wygląda na…” (The person looks…)

Task 2 — Monologue on a Given Topic (0-7 points) Express opinions or describe something. Example topics: - “What are effective methods for learning foreign languages?” - “Describe a person particularly close to you. Justify your choice.” - Advantages of city vs. countryside life - Your dream vacation

Task 3 — Dialogue with Examiner (0-6 points) Role-play scenarios. Examples: - Call a language school to inquire about courses and pricing - Call a sick friend to offer help with shopping - Make a complaint at a hotel - Arrange a meeting with a colleague

Important: Determine whether the scenario requires formal (Pan/Pani) or informal (ty) register.

Scoring Breakdown (40 points total)

Category Points
Task 1 completion 0-7
Task 2 completion 0-7
Task 3 completion 0-6
Grammar accuracy 0-8
Vocabulary & style 0-8
Pronunciation & fluency 0-4
Total 40

Candidate insight: Examiners are consistently described as friendly and supportive. The stress comes from nervousness, not from the examiners. Never stop talking, even if making errors.

3. Scoring & Passing Criteria

Points Summary

Module Max Points Pass Minimum (50%)
Listening 30 15
Reading 30 15
Grammar 30 15
Writing 30 15
Speaking 40 20
Total 160 80

Critical Rule

You must score at least 50% in EVERY module independently. Failing even one module = failing the entire exam. A 100% in reading cannot compensate for 49% in grammar.

4. Difficulty Ranking — What Real Candidates Say

Based on blog posts, forum discussions, and language school reports:

Rank Section Consensus Evidence
1 (Hardest) Grammar Universally the hardest Rated 10/10 by one candidate, barely passed at 50%. Even Polish language professionals express doubt about passing.

5. Preparation Recommendations

5.1 Overall Strategy

  1. Take a diagnostic test first. Download a free sample test from certyfikatpolski.pl, do it under timed conditions, and identify weak spots. Aim for 70%+ before booking the exam.

  2. General lessons are NOT enough. Every successful candidate stresses that exam-specific preparation is essential. The exam has specific formats and time constraints that require dedicated practice.

  3. Budget minimum 50 hours for exam-specific preparation (on top of your existing B1 knowledge).

  4. Use free official materials first before buying textbooks. The certyfikatpolski.pl website has complete sample exams with audio and answer keys.

  5. Practice writing by hand — many candidates haven’t handwritten anything in years, even in their native language.

  6. The 90/10 rule (from exam prep instructor): “My help is 10%, your independent work is 90%.”

5.2 Section-Specific Strategies

Listening

  • Drill Task 1 relentlessly — it’s played once and it’s the first thing on the exam
  • Tasks 2-5 are played twice and described as “fairly simple”
  • Listen to Polish radio, podcasts, and TV regularly to build speed comprehension
  • Recommended podcast: Polski Daily Stories, Pozdrowienia z Polski

Reading

  • Practice even if it feels like your strongest skill — “the actual test may be harder than expected”
  • Read adapted newspaper articles (Gazeta Wyborcza level)
  • Practice all task types: multiple choice, TAK/NIE, matching, text reconstruction

Grammar

  • Focus areas: all 7 cases, verb aspects, comparatives/superlatives, conditionals, prepositions + case governance
  • “There’s no getting around it” — drill repeatedly
  • The hardest subtask: choosing the correct tense AND writing the properly conjugated form
  • Do official sample grammar sections multiple times

Writing

  • Don’t try to master all text types — focus strategically on recurring ones:
    • Short form: wishes (życzenia), greetings from vacation (pozdrowienia)
    • Long form: review (recenzja), informal letter, essay (rozprawka)
  • Learn the required structure for each type (each has specific formatting requirements)
  • Memorize key connectors: po pierwsze, po drugie, z jednej strony, z drugiej strony, moim zdaniem, podsumowując
  • Practice counting words — know what 50 and 175 words look like in your handwriting
  • Use YouTube channel “Pozdrowienia z Polski” for writing templates

Speaking

  • Practice with another person — this is not optional
  • Photo description template: describe who, what, where, then give your opinion (~1 min describing + time for commentary)
  • Always determine formal (Pan/Pani) vs. informal (ty) register for Task 3
  • Speak slower when nervous — it reduces mistakes
  • Remember: examiners are friendly, the introductory small talk is not graded, and never stop talking

5.3 Timeline & Study Plan

If Already at B1 Level (Exam-Specific Prep)

When Action
3-4 months before Confirm target exam date, check registration schedule
2-3 months before Register immediately when slots open — they fill in minutes!
2 months before Start daily structured preparation (30-60 min/day minimum)
6 weeks before Work through sample exams systematically, join a prep course
3-4 weeks before Full mock exams under timed conditions, focus on weak areas
1 week before Final mock exam, light review, no cramming
Day before Stop studying — avoid mental overload

If Coming from A2

Budget 6-12 months of regular study before exam-specific prep begins.

If Starting from Zero

Approximately 350-500 hours of study (CEFR estimate for Polish). Realistic timeline: ~2 years.

Daily Study Routine (Final 2 Months)

  • Mon/Wed/Fri: Grammar drills (20 min) + Writing practice (30 min)
  • Tue/Thu: Listening exercises (20 min) + Reading practice (20 min)
  • Sat: Full mock exam section under timed conditions
  • Sun: Speaking practice with a partner/tutor
  • Daily: 15 min vocabulary with Anki/flashcards

5.4 Exam Day Tips

  1. Stop studying the day before — avoid mental overload
  2. Arrive early — registration can be slow with 130+ candidates
  3. Bring: passport, 2+ non-erasable pens (black/blue), water, snacks
  4. Expect everything in Polish — zero English on the exam
  5. Phones are collected at the door
  6. Reset mentally between sections — use the 15-20 min breaks
  7. Don’t panic about grammar — even native speakers find it hard. Focus on getting 50%+
  8. Never stop talking during the speaking section, even if making errors
  9. Count words during writing — or know what your target looks like in your handwriting

7. Registration & Logistics

2026 Exam Dates

Registration Warning

Spots fill within MINUTES of opening. The Polonization blogger reported all spots gone by 13:34. Some centers report slots disappearing in under 4 minutes.

  • Registration opens ~2 months before the exam
  • Monitor your chosen center’s website closely
  • Some schools bundle exam spots with prep courses (guaranteed spot)
  • If you miss registration, the next session may be months away — and also full

Costs

Item Amount
Exam fee (adults, B1) up to 150 EUR (~700 PLN)
Certificate issuance (if you pass) 20 EUR (~100 PLN)
Youth exam (14-17) 90 EUR

What to Bring

  • Valid passport
  • 2+ non-erasable pens (black or blue ink)
  • Water
  • Snacks (6+ hour exam day)

8. Alternative: TELC Exam

Since 2023, TELC Polish B1-B2 is recognized for EU long-term residence (but NOT for Polish citizenship).

Feature State Exam TELC
Grammar 8 tasks, 45 min standalone section Only 2 grammar tasks
Writing Multiple format types, handwritten Semi-formal email only
Results 2-4 months 4-5 weeks
Registration Extremely competitive More available
Recognized for Citizenship + EU residence EU residence only
Level B1 only B1-B2 in one sitting

If you need the certificate only for EU long-term residence (not citizenship), TELC may be a faster and less grammar-intensive path.
